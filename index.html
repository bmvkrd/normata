Who it's for

Built for the people who run teams of five to fifteen.

High cognitive load, limited time, and a stack of tools that don't talk to each other.

Primary
Engineering managers
Managing 5–15 engineers across one or two teams. Signals replaces the manual pattern-checking that slips between 1:1s.
Primary
Tech leads & team leads
Owning rituals, code review health, and on‑call. Signals surfaces what's falling through without adding another tool to check.
Primary

Integrations

Core categories. Read‑only.

Connect your day‑to‑day tools and pick the signals you care about from the catalog.



Collaboration tools

Public channel activity, thread health, and communication patterns — surfaced when something shifts from a person's normal baseline.



Calendars

1:1 cadence, meeting load, and recurring ritual health — tracked passively from the manager's own calendar grant.



Code tools

PR review health, cycle time, after‑hours merge patterns, and code review quality.

AI‑powered

Built on language, not just rules.

AI is what fuses Signals into one clear picture of one person.



Cross‑source fusion

A quiet week, two stalled PRs, and a missed 1:1 each look unremarkable in isolation. AI correlates them across categories into a single briefing about one person — something no SQL rule can produce.



Substance over surface

Language models score things rules can't: whether a PR review was substantive or rubber‑stamped, whether a Slack message is a real blocker or background chatter, whether a PR description explains why.



Manager‑shaped framing

Every briefing is written for you. Evidence is specific, framing is non‑prescriptive, and your judgment is always the last step. Signals never acts on your behalf.

AI processing runs inside your org's scope boundary — data is redacted before it reaches any model, never used to rank individuals or compare across teams. Bring your own model config, or skip AI framing entirely.
